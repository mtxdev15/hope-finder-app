# Declare and Believe — Project Brief
*Living document. Update as the app evolves.*

---

## App Identity

**Name:** Declare and Believe  
**Tagline:** Renew your mind with Scripture — instantly, personally, powerfully.  
**Core Scripture:** Romans 12:2 — *"Be transformed by the renewing of your mind."*  
**Category:** Christian faith app / Bible tool / Mind renewal / Spiritual wellness  

---

## What It Is

A single-page web app for Christians who arrive feeling stuck in a specific thought pattern or mindset struggle. The visitor describes what they're battling — by typing it in their own words or selecting from a list of common struggles — and the app instantly returns a personalized, faith-filled response rooted in Scripture.

No ads. No blog posts. No fluff. Just God's Word, directly applied to their exact situation.

---

## What the App Delivers

For every struggle submitted, the user receives:

1. **3–5 relevant Bible verses** (KJV or NLT, or both)
2. **A short faith-based explanation** — how Scripture speaks to that specific mindset and how to overcome it through Christ
3. **A personalized prayer** the visitor can read silently or aloud
4. **A declaration** — an "I am" or "I declare" statement rooted in the verse they can speak over themselves

---

## Core Goal

Help the user shift from their current broken or fearful identity into their true identity in Christ — renewing the mind as described in Romans 12:2. The app is not a counseling tool or a content platform. It is a mind renewal tool. One interaction. One moment. God's Word for their exact situation.

---

## Brand Voice

**Warm. Direct. Pastoral. Confident. Spirit-led.**

Like a trusted friend who knows their Bible deeply. Never preachy. Never clinical. Never hedging. Always pointing back to identity in Christ.

The voice names the lie, speaks the truth, and leaves the person standing in who God says they are — not who their circumstances say they are.

---

## Declaration Style — Match This Voice Exactly

These are the tonal and structural benchmarks for every declaration the app generates:

- "I am not defined by my past. I am defined by my Creator."
- "I am not moved by what I see. I am moved by what God said."
- "I am in control of my thoughts because the mind of Christ is mine."
- "I am covered by the blood of Jesus Christ."
- "God did not give me a spirit of fear — He gave me power, love, and a sound mind."
- "I am who God says I am — not what my circumstances say."
- "I am the husband my wife prayed for."
- "I am the father my child needs."
- "I am fearless. Fear is not my portion."
- "I am filled with wisdom and I move with it."

### Declaration DNA — Three Non-Negotiables

1. **Contrast structure** — Name the lie, then defeat it. "I am not X. I am Y." The push-pull between the broken identity and the true identity does real spiritual work.
2. **Specificity** — Declarations must connect to what the user actually described. "I am the husband my wife prayed for" hits harder than "I am loved." The more specific, the more powerful.
3. **Present tense only** — Never "I am becoming" or "I am learning to." The declaration is settled, done, and already true in the spirit. This reflects Romans 4:17 — calling things that are not as though they are.

## Target Audience

### Primary — Who Arrives First

| Persona | Description | Their 3am Search |
|---|---|---|
| **The silently struggling Christian** | Ages 18–45. Functional on the outside, battling a quiet inner war. Won't call a pastor at 3am but will open a search bar. | "Why do I still feel like this if I have faith?" |
| **The burned-out believer** | Ages 25–50. Has been through something hard. Still believes but feels disconnected from God. Needs Scripture without condemnation. | "I know the verses. I just can't feel them right now." |
| **The young adult adrift** | Ages 18–34. Grew up in church, drifted. Loneliest generation in recorded history. Needs a non-judgmental voice without church culture baggage. | "Does God still have something to say to someone like me?" |
| **The nighttime crisis visitor** | Any age. Arrived via a desperate search. May not identify as Christian. Needs compassion first, Scripture second, crisis resources if needed. | "I just need someone to tell me it gets better." |

### Secondary — Daytime Intentional Users
- Morning devotional builders
- Small group and Bible study leaders
- Christian counselors and prayer ministers
- Christian parents guiding struggling teens
- People in recovery and healing journeys

### The Universal Thread
They all share the same gap: they know God exists but can't find his voice for their specific pain right now. They have a Bible but don't know where to turn in it. They need one clear Word from God — and permission to claim it as their own.

---

## What the App Is NOT

- Not a sermon or devotional content platform
- Not a mental health app or counseling substitute
- Not a church bulletin or Bible study tool
- Not preachy, clinical, or lecture-based
- Not aspirational — it does not tell people who they can become. It tells them who they already are in Christ.

## Key Research Insights (Inform Build Decisions)

- Google confirmed anxiety search interest peaks at **2am**
- Depression-related searches rise sharply from **6pm to midnight**
- **30%** of Americans aged 18–34 report feeling lonely every day
- **20%** of US adults have no close friends outside of family (up from 3% in 1990)
- Late-night searching is described by researchers as "confession typed into a blank search bar" — it is private, unfiltered, and vulnerable
- The cognitive controls that minimize fear are weakened at night — what felt manageable at 2pm feels catastrophic at 3am
- Crisis-tier keywords ("I have no reason to live") trigger Google's protected results — an app with compassionate landing language and a 988 mention can rank in that space
