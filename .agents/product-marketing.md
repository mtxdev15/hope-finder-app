# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-06-18*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** Describe what you're battling and instantly receive God's Word for it — Scripture, a prayer, and an identity declaration you can speak over yourself.
**What it does:** Declare & Believe is a faith-based web app for Christians stuck in a specific mindset struggle (fear, shame, failure, loneliness, doubt). The visitor names what they're carrying, by typing it or picking from a list, and the app returns 3 relevant Bible verses (NKJV/NLT/NIV), a short pastoral explanation, a personalized prayer, and present-tense "I am / I declare" statements rooted in the verse. One interaction, one moment, God's Word for their exact situation. Built on Romans 12:2.
**Product category:** Christian faith app / Bible tool / mind renewal. We sit in a **transformation-and-freedom lane** (getting unstuck in a single moment), not the daily-habit devotional lane.
**Product type:** Web app (Astro + Cloudflare + Anthropic Claude + Convex). Free accounts that sync a saved "vault," profile, and a guided journey across devices.
**Business model:** Free, no ads, no paywall. Funded by donations (the Give page; Stripe is the next build). Owner: JC Kingdom Ventures, LLC.

## Target Audience
**Who it's for:** Christian men and women (equally) carrying a private inner struggle, who won't call a pastor at 3am but will open a search bar. Functional on the outside, fighting a quiet war inside.
**Primary use case:** The 2am-to-4am private moment when a thought pattern has someone stuck and they need one clear Word from God for their exact pain, right now.
**Jobs to be done:**
- When I'm overwhelmed by fear/shame/failure at night, give me God's truth for *this* feeling so I'm not alone with it.
- When I know God exists but can't find His voice for my pain, point me to the exact Scripture and let me claim it.
- When my identity feels broken, tell me who I already am in Christ (not who I could become).
**Use cases / scenarios:** late-night crisis search; morning devotional anchor; a man comparing himself to other men and feeling behind; a burned-out believer who "knows the verses but can't feel them"; a young adult who drifted from church and wants a non-judgmental voice; recovery and healing journeys; small-group leaders and Christian parents guiding someone struggling.

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** They have a Bible but don't know where to turn in it for the specific thing crushing them right now, and the cognitive controls that keep fear manageable are weakest at night.
**Why alternatives fall short:**
- General Bible apps make you already know the verse you need.
- Search engines and devotionals give content to scroll, not a Word for *this* moment.
- A pastor or friend isn't reachable (or safe to be vulnerable with) at 3am.
**What it costs them:** Another sleepless night believing the lie; faith that feels distant; shame carried in silence.
**Emotional tension:** Worthlessness, fear, shame, loneliness, "God has forgotten me." Private, unfiltered, vulnerable — "confession typed into a blank search bar."

## Competitive Landscape
We compete for the **moment of struggle**, not the daily habit. Our real rivals are what a hurting person does at 2am instead of opening us.

**Direct (what they do at 2am instead of us):** Googling "Bible verses for anxiety" and skimming a generic list; scrolling social or a Christian blog; asking ChatGPT; or just lying there alone with the lie. These fall short: generic, impersonal, no pastoral identity voice, nothing to speak aloud, no crisis care — they leave the person stuck.
**Adjacent (faith-habit platforms, NOT direct competition):** Hallow, YouVersion / Bible.com, Pray.com, Abide, Glorify. Built for daily habit and breadth (reading plans, meditation, prayer routines). They serve a different job; they are not built for getting unstuck from one specific struggle in the moment.
**Indirect (different approach):** Mental-health / affirmation apps (Calm, Headspace, I Am), journaling, calling a friend or pastor. Fall short: not Scripture-rooted; affirmations point to self, not to who God says you are.

## Differentiation
**Category framing:** transformation and freedom — getting unstuck in a moment — not maintaining a habit.
**Key differentiators:**
- Struggle → Scripture → declaration → prayer in one instant, personalized response.
- **Identity, not aspiration:** tells you who you *already are* in Christ, never who you could become.
- **Present-tense declarations** with contrast structure ("I am not X, I am Y") you speak aloud.
- 3am-ready and crisis-aware (988 protocol), compassionate landing language.
- No ads, no content to scroll, no paywall.
**Why customers choose us:** It meets the exact moment of struggle with God's Word made personal and speakable, and moves them from stuck to free — not a library to search or a habit to keep.

## Objections
| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "Is this just AI putting words in God's mouth?" | Every response is grounded in actual Scripture (3 verses, real references); the app applies the Word, it doesn't replace it. |
| "Is this a counseling/mental-health tool?" | No. It's a mind-renewal tool, not therapy. For crisis it surfaces the 988 line and never refuses to engage. |
| "I've tried 'just give it to God' and it didn't work." | This doesn't hand you a platitude; it gives you the specific Word for your specific battle and a declaration to stand on. |

**Anti-persona:** Someone wanting a full Bible-study platform, sermon library, or social community; someone seeking clinical treatment; anyone looking for content to browse rather than a Word for the moment.

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** Sleepless nights, shame carried silently, faith that feels far, generic verse lists that don't land.
**Pull:** Instant, personal Scripture for *this* struggle, plus a declaration to speak and a prayer to pray right now.
**Habit:** Defaulting to Google, a familiar Bible app, or scrolling; or just lying awake with the thought.
**Anxiety:** "Will this feel preachy or fake?" "Is it safe to be this honest?" Addressed by warmth, privacy, and no condemnation.

## Customer Language
**How they describe the problem (verbatim from research):**
- "Why do I still feel like this if I have faith?"
- "I know the verses. I just can't feel them right now."
- "Does God still have something to say to someone like me?"
- "I keep comparing myself to other men my age and feel like I'm behind in life and God has forgotten about me."
**How they describe what they want:** "I just need someone to tell me it gets better." One clear Word from God, and permission to claim it as their own.
**Words to use:** declare, believe, renew your mind, identity in Christ, who God says you are, the Word, Scripture for what you're carrying, the weight you carry, stuck, free, unstuck.
**Words to avoid:** clinical/therapy framing, "becoming" or "learning to" (declarations are present-tense and settled), preachy church-culture jargon, hype/marketing buzzwords. **No em dashes in marketing prose** (house style).
**Glossary:**
| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| A "Word" | One full response: 3 verses + explanation + declarations + prayer |
| Declaration | A present-tense "I am / I declare" statement rooted in the verse |
| The Vault | A user's saved verses, declarations, prayers, and collections (synced to their account) |
| Journey | The guided multi-day mind-renewal path |
| Struggle pages | The 13 SEO landing pages (anxiety, shame, failure, etc.) |

**Personality:** Trustworthy, grounded, reverent, calm, bold.
