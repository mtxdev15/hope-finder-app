05 — Voice & Tone

Brand Voice

✓ We Are

Warm and direct — like a trusted friend who knows their Bible
Grounded in identity in Christ, not positive thinking
Confident and Spirit-led — speaking truth with authority
Specific to the man's exact struggle — never generic
Present tense — "I am" not "I will be"
Faith-forward — pointing back to God's Word always

✗ We Are Not

Preachy, clinical, or religious-sounding
Generic motivational content ("you've got this!")
Distant or formal — no liturgy, no stiff language
Repetitive — prayers never use the same phrases twice
Future-focused declarations ("I will be free someday")
Therapy substitute — always point to God, not self-help

Declaration Voice Examples

I am not defined by my past. I am defined by my Creator.

I am not moved by what I see. I am moved by what God said.

I declare that fear is not my portion. God gave me power, love, and a sound mind.

I am who God says I am — not what my circumstances say.

I am the man. The husband. The father. The creator. The son of God.

I am living in the promise. Right now. Today.

06 — Art Direction

Visual Mood

Warm morning light. Open Bible. Man in quiet reflection.
WARM · DEVOTIONAL
Deep forest. Stillness. A man standing in faith — alone but not alone.
DEEP · GROUNDED
Golden hour. New beginning. Light breaking through.
LIGHT · HOPEFUL

Photography and illustrations should feel warm, masculine, and sacred — not stock-photo generic. Men in quiet strength. Morning light. Scripture. Never prosperity-gospel imagery (flashy wealth, big stages). The visual world of Declare & Believe is a sanctuary, not a conference.

The layout favors generous breathing room — a calming, sanctuary-like spaciousness. Max content width: 760px centered. Mobile-first, fluid typography using clamp().

08 — Usage Rules

Do's & Don'ts

✓ Correct

Declare & Believe

"Believe" always italic in brand contexts

✗ Incorrect

Declare and Believe

Never spell out the ampersand in the logo

✓ Correct

I am who God says I am.

Present tense. Identity-rooted. First person.

✗ Incorrect

I will become who God wants me to be someday.

Never future tense. You are already in Christ.

✓ Correct

Forest green on cream. Gold on forest. White on forest.

Approved color pairings only

✗ Incorrect

Gold text on cream background.

Gold on cream fails accessibility contrast
