# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Christians ages 18–45 who are struggling silently — fear, shame, broken identity, loneliness,
burnout, doubt. The archetypal user arrives at 2–4am on their phone at their lowest point.
Secondary users: morning devotional readers, Bible study leaders, parents guiding teens,
people in recovery. All share one thread: they know God exists but can't find His voice
for their specific pain right now.

## Product Purpose

Declare & Believe is a faith-based mind renewal tool built on Romans 12:2. A user describes
their struggle — by selecting a chip or writing it out — and receives: three targeted Bible
verses (in their chosen translation), a pastoral explanation of how Scripture speaks to that
pain, 3–5 personalized declarations rooted in Scripture, and a prayer they can speak aloud.

Success looks like: a person leaves the interaction knowing who they are in Christ. Not just
informed — transformed. Every word choice, animation, and color exists to serve that moment.

## Brand Personality

Pastoral · Grounded · Tender

The voice is a trusted friend who knows the Bible cold and meets you where you are — no
judgment, all truth. Never preachy. Never therapeutic in the secular sense. Never shallow.
Speaks with the quiet authority of someone who has been in the dark too and found the Word
to be a lamp.

## Anti-references

1. **Therapy / wellness apps** (Calm, Headspace, BetterHelp): Secular minimalism, soft gradients,
   "science-backed" language. Dilutes the spiritual edge. Declare & Believe is explicitly
   Christian — the cross, the Holy Spirit, the Word — not wellness-adjacent.

2. **Instagram devotionals**: Pastel backgrounds, cursive fonts, gold foil verse quotes. Pretty
   but shallow. Feels like decoration, not truth. The app should feel like a serious encounter
   with Scripture, not a motivational poster.

## Design Principles

1. **Meet them where they are.** The 3am user is exhausted and fragile. Every interaction must
   feel like someone is already on the other side waiting — no friction, no learning curve.

2. **Scripture is sacred. UI is invisible.** The Word gets serif, weight, and space. Interface
   chrome recedes. The goal is for the user to encounter God's voice, not our design.

3. **Specificity over comfort.** Declarations should feel targeted, not generic. The experience
   should feel personal — not like a vending machine that dispenses Bible verses.

4. **Mobile first, always.** The 3am user is on their phone. Every pixel decision is a mobile
   decision first.

5. **Silence the noise.** No distractions, no ads, no notifications, no rabbit holes. One screen.
   One struggle. One Word.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum on all text. Current implementation is AA/AAA across all tokens. Generous
line heights (1.6–1.8) for late-night anxious reading. Touch targets minimum 44px. Color is
never the only differentiator (state, focus, error use shape + label too). Reduced motion
must be respected for the cross loader pulse and results fade-up animations.
