Product & Engineering Spec

Church data & the Claim flow

Owner  ProductStatus  Draft for sign-offUpdated  June 2026

Google can tell us where a church is. Only the church can tell us when it gathers and whether it streams. This spec decides who owns that data — and how we collect it without drowning your team.

Recommendation

Go hybrid, in phases. Seed a launch region with team-curated data so the map is never empty, then open a self-serve “Claim your church” flow so churches maintain their own service times and online status. Discovery stays 100% Google — we only ever store the two things Google can’t give us: service times and online/livestream status.

The product

The “Claim your church” flow

Reached from any church profile via a quiet “Is this your church?” link. Five steps, mobile-first, in Declare’s voice.

Find & select the church
They search the same Google-backed directory and pick their church. If it already has an owner, we route to “suggest an edit” instead.
Verify ownership
The trust gate. They prove they represent the church via one of the methods below before anything publishes.
Gate — nothing goes live until this passes
Add the details
A short, friendly form: service times (day + time rows), the online toggle + livestream link, language, and an optional welcome blurb. This is the data the app has been missing.
Review & submit
A preview of exactly how their profile will look in Declare. One tap to submit; status becomes pending.
Published
On approval (auto for trusted verification, else a quick admin pass) it goes live — with the gold success moment we already built. They get an editor link to keep it fresh.
Re-confirm every 6 months to stay “verified”

Engineering

The thin backend

Small and boring on purpose. One read endpoint the app already calls, plus the write/verify surface for Claim and admin. The app stays provider-agnostic — see CHURCH-API-INTEGRATION.md.

GET/enrich?placeIds=…App reads merged data (built)
POST/claimsStart a claim for a placeId
POST/claims/:id/verifySubmit email/phone/postcard code
POST/claims/:id/profileSave service times, online, blurb
GET/admin/queuePending claims & edit suggestions

Data store

A single church_profiles table keyed by placeId covers everything — the claimed fields, an owners list, status, and an edits audit trail. No need for a heavyweight CMS.

Internal tooling

The admin (kept minimal)

  • Seed editor — search a place, type its service times + online status. This is the Phase 1 launch tool.
  • Claim queue — approve / reject pending claims that didn’t auto-verify; one-click approve for domain-email matches.
  • Edit suggestions — lightweight inbox for “suggest an edit” from non-owners (wrong time, closed, etc.).
  • Freshness flags — surface profiles past their 6-month re-confirm so they can be nudged or de-verified.
Trust & freshness

Keeping it honest

  • Nothing publishes unverified. Step 2 is a hard gate; pending data never reaches the app.
  • Verified badge with a half-life. Owners re-confirm every 6 months; lapse drops the badge, not the data.
  • Crowd corrections. Any user can “suggest an edit” — queued, never auto-applied.
  • One owner, many editors. A church can add staff without re-verifying each person.
  • Audit trail. Every change is attributed and reversible.
Sequencing

How we roll it out

Phase 1
Seed & launch
Google discovery goes live. Team curates service times + online for the launch region via the seed editor. Users get a complete map immediately. No claim flow yet.
Phase 2
Open the Claim flow
Ship “Is this your church?” with domain-email + phone verification. Churches start maintaining their own profiles; coverage expands beyond the seed region on its own.
Phase 3
Self-sustaining
Add postcard verification, edit suggestions, and freshness nudges. The directory now grows and stays current with little team effort. Events stay out of scope.
