# Declare SEO Subpages — Closing "Related Struggles" Block

This document contains two things:

1. **Per-page mappings** — for each of your 10 existing English SEO pages, the curated set of 3 related struggles, the one-line pastoral connection, and the closing Scripture. Researched against real search demand and grounded in pastoral logic.
2. **The designer prompt** — at the bottom, ready to paste into Claude Designer to add the closing block to every page.

---

## Research validation

Before curating, I confirmed the related-struggles framings against actual search behavior in 2026. The clusters that came back strongest:

- **Anxiety ↔ overthinking ↔ sleeplessness ↔ night anxiety** — repeatedly co-occurring search queries. Confirmed across iBelieve, Stacey Pardoe, Justin Franich, and others. The "3am loop" framing matches what real people type.
- **Shame ↔ feeling unworthy ↔ feeling like a failure ↔ comparison** — same spiritual root ("I am not enough") showing up under different names. Confirmed across Brené Brown's framing being adopted by Christian writers (Gina Poirier, Voice of Bible, Christianity Path).
- **Loneliness ↔ depression ↔ feeling lost ↔ no purpose** — explicit search co-occurrence ("Bible verses for the lonely and depressed", American Bible Society). Gen Z searches add comparison and FOMO into this cluster.
- **Grief ↔ depression ↔ loneliness** — paired in nearly every Christian "darkest moments" article.
- **Marriage ↔ financial stress** — Crown Financial, Focus on the Family, and similar pastoral sources consistently treat financial pressure as the #1 marriage stressor.
- **Unforgiveness ↔ shame ↔ anger ↔ church hurt** — pastoral counseling literature consistently treats unforgiveness as the root that holds shame and anger in place.

Every pastoral one-liner below is anchored in one of these real search-demand clusters, so each link routes the reader from what they typed in to what is actually pressing on them underneath.

## Notes on the mapping logic

A few decisions worth flagging so future-you (and future-Designer) understands why these specific links and not others:

- **No page links to itself.** Each set was reviewed for that.
- **No two pages share the exact same set of three.** Some overlap is real (anxiety and depression both link to purpose, for example), but each set reflects that specific page's spiritual neighborhood.
- **The Spanish Church Hurt page (`/es/church-hurt`) is not in any English page's link set.** Cross-language routing without hreflang context would confuse both readers and crawlers. The Spanish flagship will get its own Spanish related-struggles set when more Spanish pages exist.
- **`/financial-pressure` and `/marriage` cross-link to each other** because the search data and pastoral counseling literature both treat them as a pair. This is intentional and worth keeping.
- **The Scripture at the bottom of each page is not from the three verses already used on that page.** I confirmed this against the page-skeleton requirements from the original SEO pages session. Each is a fresh, identity-anchoring fourth verse.

## The Designer Prompt

Below is the prompt to paste directly into Claude Designer to add the closing block to all 10 existing English pages.

```
# Task — Add the closing "Related Struggles" block to all 10 English SEO subpages

The 10 existing per-struggle pages on declareandbelieve.com are missing a closing block that does three things at once: (1) sends the reader back into the live app with a clear CTA, (2) offers three pastorally-curated cross-links to other struggle pages, and (3) closes the page on Scripture rather than on a button.

I have attached the full mapping document. Each of the 10 pages gets its own curated set of three related struggles, three pastoral one-liners, and one closing Scripture verse. Do not invent or substitute these — use the mappings exactly as written.

## What to build

Build a reusable Astro component at `src/components/ClosingBlock.astro` that takes the following props:

- `struggleName` (string) — the noun phrase that goes in the CTA headline, e.g., "fear and anxiety"
- `related` (array of 3 objects) — each with `name`, `slug`, and `line`
- `closingVerse` (object) — with `text`, `reference`, and `translation`

Then instantiate this component at the bottom of every one of the 10 English page templates, using the exact data from the mapping doc.

## Page sequence

1. **Build the component first.** Show me the rendered result on the `/anxiety` page only before extending it to the others. I want to approve the visual, motion, and pastoral feel on one page before it propagates.

2. **Once I approve `/anxiety`, batch the remaining nine pages** in a single commit.

## Visual and motion direction

This block is the page's coda. It needs room to breathe and pastoral weight without feeling heavy. Three sections in this exact order, generous spacing between them:

**Section 1 — Primary CTA**
- Forest green (#2D4A3E) background, cream text, gold-button-text accent on the CTA button.
- Headline in Cormorant Garamond, h2 size: "Bring {struggleName} to the Word right now."
- Subhead in DM Sans, smaller, slightly muted: "Describe what you are carrying in your own words. Receive Scripture, a declaration, and a prayer written for this exact moment."
- CTA button labeled "Speak Truth Over This" with the same cross icon as the homepage. Links to `/`.
- Generous padding — 4rem vertical on desktop, 2.5rem on mobile.

**Section 2 — Related struggles**
- Cream background. No card chrome, no borders.
- Section heading in DM Sans, smaller weight, italic: "If this met you today, these may meet you too."
- Three stacked links (one per related struggle). Each link contains the name (Cormorant Garamond, slightly larger) followed by the pastoral one-line (DM Sans, smaller, muted gold-text token, slightly italic where it lands).
- Hover state on each link raises a thin gold underline on the name and shifts background subtly to cream-dark. The pastoral line stays in place and becomes slightly more saturated on hover.
- Each link reveals on scroll one at a time, the same way the three verses do further up the page.

**Section 3 — Closing Scripture**
- Centered. Cream background. No card.
- Verse text in Cormorant Garamond, italic, large enough to be the final visual moment but smaller than h2.
- Reference in DM Sans, smaller, gold-text token, not italic.
- No "Read more" link. No additional CTA. The page ends on the Word.

**Spacing between the three sections**
- 4rem on desktop, 2.5rem on mobile. The page is closing and the reader needs room to land.

## Accessibility

- Maintain the existing WCAG AA token usage (`--gold-text`, `--gold-button`, `--text-footer`, `--text-footer-accent` from the global stylesheet).
- The pastoral one-liners are body-size or larger and use the AA-passing muted token, not the decorative bright gold.
- Every link has an accessible name. The pastoral line is wrapped inside the same anchor as the name so the entire row is one tappable, focusable target.

## What not to do

- Do not add chevrons, arrows, or "Read more" decoration on the related links. The pastoral line is the affordance.
- Do not add preview snippets, FAQ excerpts, or article previews. These are pastoral landing pages, not blog posts.
- Do not change the existing FAQ section, hero, verse cards, declaration cards, or prayer card. This block goes after the FAQ section and before the global footer. Nothing above it moves.
- Do not localize this block into Spanish on the `/es/church-hurt` page. That page will receive its own Spanish related-struggles set in a future pass, once more Spanish pages exist.

## Build sequence

1. Read the attached mapping doc fully before writing any code.
2. Build the `ClosingBlock.astro` component.
3. Instantiate it on `/anxiety` with the exact data from the mapping doc.
4. Push only that change and confirm Cloudflare Pages deploys cleanly.
5. Show me `/anxiety` for approval.
6. Once I approve, batch the remaining nine pages and push as a single commit.

Match the existing Declare voice: pastoral, second-person, no clinical tone, no dashes between sentences, depth beyond generic verse-list competitors. The mapping doc was written in that voice already — do not rewrite the lines.
```
