Bible Verses & Prayer for Anxiety

When worry presses in, God’s Word steadies the heart. Here is scripture, a way to overcome the anxious mind, declarations to speak, and a prayer for peace.

What does the Bible say about anxiety? Again and again, Scripture tells us not to be ruled by anxiety — not because our fears aren’t real, but because God is near and He cares for us. Philippians 4:6-7 invites us to pray about everything and receive a peace that guards the heart, and 1 Peter 5:7 tells us to cast all our anxiety on Him, because He cares for us.

Scripture for Anxiety

“For God has not given us a spirit of fear, but of power and of love and of a sound mind.”

2 Timothy 1:7

“Be anxious for nothing, but in everything by prayer and supplication, with thanksgiving, let your requests be made known to God; and the peace of God, which surpasses all understanding, will guard your hearts and minds through Christ Jesus.”

Philippians 4:6-7

“Fear not, for I am with you; be not dismayed, for I am your God. I will strengthen you, yes, I will help you, I will uphold you with My righteous right hand.”

Isaiah 41:10
Overcome the Anxious Mind

A way to overcome anxiety

Anxiety grows in the dark of “what if,” and panic can make the moment feel bigger than God. But you were not handed a spirit of fear — you carry power, love, and a sound mind. Peace is not found by thinking harder; it’s found by bringing the worry to God and fixing your thoughts on Him. When the racing starts, His peace is nearer than your next breath.

“I Am” Declarations

Speak these over your anxiety

  • “I am not ruled by fear — I am held by the One who calms storms.
  • “I am of a sound mind; peace guards my heart and my thoughts.
  • “I am safe in His care, today and in all that is ahead.
A Prayer for Anxiety

Pray this today

Father, I bring You this fear and I let it go. You have not made me to live anxious — You have given me power, love, and a sound mind. Quiet what races in me and guard my heart with Your peace. I trust You with what I cannot control. Amen.

Questions People Ask

What does the Bible say about anxiety?

Scripture repeatedly tells us not to be ruled by anxiety because God is with us and cares for us. Philippians 4:6-7 invites us to pray about everything and receive a peace that guards our hearts, and 1 Peter 5:7 says to cast all our anxiety on Him because He cares for us.

What is a good prayer for anxiety?

“Father, I bring You this fear and I let it go. You have given me power, love, and a sound mind. Quiet what races in me and guard my heart with Your peace. I trust You with what I cannot control. Amen.”

Which Bible verse is best for anxiety and fear?

Many find Isaiah 41:10 and 2 Timothy 1:7 most comforting. Isaiah 41:10 says, “Do not fear, for I am with you,” and 2 Timothy 1:7 reminds us that God gave us a spirit not of fear but of power, love, and a sound mind.

How can I overcome anxiety as a Christian?

Bring the worry to God in prayer, replace anxious thoughts with Scripture, and speak truth over yourself out loud. Anxiety grows in “what if”; peace grows as you fix your mind on God and trust Him with what you cannot control.
